01 — The brand story

Water and warmth, one neighbour

Most Montreal plumbers fix your pipes. Most HVAC techs heat your home. Boileau is certified to do both — one licensed neighbour for everything that flows or warms a Montreal home, from a triplex in Rosemont to a small commercial space on a corner in Villeray.

The name carries it. Boileau reads as "bouille l'eau" — boil the water. Water and heat have always lived together in this name. The brand mark makes that literal: a flame inside a water drop. We never have to explain who we are; the name and mark do it in one glance.

The brand's promise is built for how Montrealers actually hire trades: they verify the RBQ licence, they ask who's insured, they ask their neighbours. Boileau leans into that. We are the plumber people wait for — calm, certified, recommended over fences and in alley conversations.

Positioning statement

For Montreal homeowners, condo owners and small commercial clients who want one trusted, certified expert instead of a rotation of strangers, Plomberie Boileau is the neighbourhood master of water and warmth — RBQ-licensed in both plumbing and heating — whose work is worth the wait.

Brand personality

Warm

Talks like a good neighbour, not a call centre. Knows the alley, the borough, the building type.

Certified-calm

Never fearmongers. Explains, prevents, reassures. The licence does the flexing.

Honest about time

Booked weeks out and says so plainly. Scarcity is proof, never a sales trick.

02 — Who we speak to

Three Montreal audiences

The plex owner

Owns a duplex/triplex in Rosemont, Villeray, the Plateau, NDG. Century-old building, cast-iron stacks, hidden shut-off valves, no AC upstairs in July.

Deep fear: sewer backup in the basement during a summer storm. Quiet dream: a heat pump and a rebate cheque.

The condo owner

Insurance demands a water heater under 10–12 years old. Terrified of a leak reaching the unit below. Wants paperwork, conformity, proof.

Buys on: documentation and tidiness. Hates: vague quotes.

The small commercial / property manager

Runs a café, dépanneur, or a handful of rental units. Needs one reliable number, fast invoices, and a trade who shows up when promised.

Buys on: reliability and relationship, not price.

03 — The logo system

The crest and the mark

The primary logo is a heritage seal — the visual language of licensed Quebec trades and official conformity stamps. Inside it sits the brand mark: a flame within a water drop. Boil + eau.

Primary crest — use on truck doors, invoices, uniforms, site footer, social avatars.

Horizontal lockup — website header, email signature, documents.

Rules

  • Clear space: keep empty space around the crest equal to the height of the letter "P" in the wordmark.
  • Minimum sizes: crest 28 px / 18 mm; horizontal lockup 140 px / 45 mm wide.
  • On dark spruce: use the crest as-is (it carries its own background). The horizontal lockup switches its wordmark to ice (#F2F6F3).
  • One-colour version: all elements in deep spruce, or all in ice on dark — for stamps, embroidery, engraving.

Never

  • Never stretch, rotate, recolour, or add shadows/glows to the crest.
  • Never place the crest on busy photography without a solid circle behind it.
  • Never separate the flame from the drop — the combined glyph is the brand.

04 — Colour

Fresh trade green

Green is the working colour of Quebec's energy transition — LogisVert, Chauffez Vert, Roulez vert. Boileau's spruce green ties the trade to that movement while standing apart from the sea of blue-and-red plumber brands. Brass is the metal of valves and fittings: the warm, human accent.

Accessibility

  • Ice text on spruce: contrast 4.9:1 — passes AA for all text. Ice on deep spruce: 9.8:1.

  • Proportion guide: 60% ice/white, 25% spruce family, 10% mint, 5% brass.

06 — Voice & tone

How Boileau talks

Like a neighbour who happens to hold the licence. Plain words, borough names, zero panic, zero jargon-flexing. We explain the why, we respect people's intelligence, and we are honest about the wait.

Principles with examples

"We offer comprehensive hydronic heating solutions leveraging industry-leading technology.""We install and repair hot-water heating — boilers, radiators, heated floors. The systems older Montreal homes were built around."
"ACT NOW! Sewer backups can DESTROY your home!!""Montreal storms can push the sewer back up your basement drain. A backwater valve stops it. Ten minutes to check yours — here's how."
"Due to unprecedented demand, availability is limited.""Honestly: we're booked for the next few weeks. Join the list and we'll call you in order — no queue-jumping, no surprises."

Vocabulary

  • Say "your building," "your plex," "your block" — never "the property."
  • Name boroughs and realities: alleys, spiral staircases, cast-iron stacks, January cold snaps.
  • Numbers over adjectives: "licensed since [year]," "up to $7,840 in LogisVert rebates" — not "amazing service."
  • French words live naturally in the English voice: plex, dépanneur, RBQ, CMMTQ.

08 — Trust signals

The licence does the talking

In Quebec, trust is verifiable. These signals appear consistently — same order, same wording — on the website footer, invoices, estimates, truck, and business card:

RBQ licence № [XXXX-XXXX-XX] CMMTQ member Fully insured Plumbing + heating certified

  • The RBQ number is always shown in full and always findable within one scroll — consumers are taught to verify it in the registry, so make verification effortless.
  • Invite verification: "Look us up in the RBQ registry — we'll wait." Confidence is the brand.
  • Every estimate is written; every invoice lists licence number and insurance. Paper is part of the product.

09 — Applications

Where the brand lives

Truck

Spruce body or white body with spruce panel. Crest on both doors. Horizontal lockup + RBQ number on rear and sides in ice. Brass pinstripe along the body line.

Business card

Ice front: horizontal lockup, name, trade titles. Deep spruce back: crest centred, RBQ/CMMTQ line in brass at the foot.

Workwear

Deep spruce tee/softshell, one-colour ice crest embroidered on chest. Brass stitching detail optional. Clean boots-on look — customers photograph what shows up.

Invoice / estimate

Horizontal lockup top-left; licence badges in footer; mint panel for totals. Always a plain-language line describing what was done and why.

10 — Bilingual rules

French first, by law and by heart

Quebec's Charter of the French Language (Law 96) requires French to be at least as prominent as any other language in public-facing communications. The Boileau brand treats this as identity, not compliance:

  • The website defaults to French; English is one click away (EN toggle, top right).
  • Tagline pair is fixed: «Votre eau. Votre chaleur. Votre voisin.» / "Your water. Your warmth. Your neighbour."
  • French copy is written in Quebec French — never translated France-French. It says "plex," "thermopompe," "fournaise," "déneigement season," the way the client's neighbours do.
  • Truck, card, and invoice lead with French; English follows where space allows.
