LogisVert Heat Pump Rebate in Quebec: Get Up to $7,840 — With an RBQ Contractor

Hydro-Québec's LogisVert program pays up to approximately $7,840 toward a heat pump installation in Quebec — but the rebate only applies if the work is done by an RBQ-licensed contractor. That one condition knocks out a lot of installers, and it's the first thing to verify before you sign anything. Here's how the program works, what systems qualify, and what the process looks like from assessment to rebate cheque.

What LogisVert is — and what it's replacing

LogisVert is Hydro-Québec's residential energy transition program. The idea is straightforward: Quebec runs almost entirely on hydroelectric power, which is among the lowest-carbon electricity in the world. When you replace an oil furnace or electric baseboard heaters with a heat pump, you're not just cutting your energy bill — you're reducing Quebec's overall fossil fuel dependency. The province and Hydro-Québec both have incentives to accelerate that transition, which is where the rebate money comes from.

For Montreal homeowners and plex owners, the practical benefit is a cheque of up to $7,840 that offsets the cost of a heat pump installation. The actual amount depends on your current heating system, the type and size of heat pump you install, and whether it's a full replacement or a supplementary system. Rebate amounts are updated periodically, so the number to confirm is always at hydroquebec.com — but the order of magnitude hasn't changed significantly in recent years.

Who benefits most in Montreal

The households where a heat pump makes the biggest financial difference are those currently heating with oil or propane — which is still common in some Outremont and Westmount single-family homes — or those relying entirely on electric baseboard heaters, which is typical in Plateau and Rosemont condos and smaller plexes. Heat pumps are 2 to 3 times more efficient than electric baseboards at turning electricity into heat. The energy savings add up quickly over a Montreal winter.

The RBQ requirement: why most people miss this

This is the part that catches people off guard. You might find a heat pump installer who quotes you a competitive price, handles the equipment, and seems perfectly capable — but if they don't hold an RBQ licence for this type of work, your installation does not qualify for the LogisVert rebate. Period.

The RBQ (Régie du bâtiment du Québec) licences contractors in Quebec. For heat pump and HVAC installation, the relevant licence category is mechanical contractor. For any plumbing or refrigerant connections, a plumbing licence is also required. You can verify any contractor's licence status in the public RBQ registry online — and you should, before signing a contract.

Plomberie Boileau holds both certifications: plumbing and HVAC. That's not common — most plumbers don't do HVAC, and most HVAC companies don't hold a plumbing licence. For homeowners integrating a heat pump with existing plumbing or hydronic heating systems, having one licensed contractor handle both sides simplifies everything and means you're not coordinating two separate companies for what is ultimately one job.

What the dual certification means for you practically

If you have a Rosemont duplex with a boiler-based hydronic system and you want to integrate a heat pump, that project touches both heating and plumbing. If you have a Plateau condo with a mini-split and a conventional water heater, those systems interact. When the contractor holds both licences, there's one estimate, one timeline, and one person accountable for the whole picture.

Which systems qualify for LogisVert rebates

Most current heat pump systems — if they meet the efficiency ratings specified in the program — are eligible. This includes:

  • Ductless mini-split systems (single-zone and multi-zone), which are the most common choice in Rosemont, Plateau, and Mile-End duplexes and condos
  • Central heat pump systems with air distribution, more typical in Outremont and Westmount single-family homes
  • Cold-climate heat pumps rated for Quebec winters, which maintain efficiency at temperatures well below freezing

The efficiency threshold is a HSPF2 (Heating Seasonal Performance Factor) rating that Hydro-Québec specifies and updates. We confirm equipment eligibility before proposing any system — there's no point installing something that doesn't qualify.

Mini-splits in plexes: the installation reality

Ductless mini-splits have become the standard choice for Montreal plexes and condos for a practical reason: there are no ducts. In a 1920s Rosemont triplex with plaster walls and no duct space, running new ducts is prohibitively expensive and disruptive. A mini-split's indoor unit mounts on a wall, the refrigerant line set runs through a small hole to the outdoor compressor, and you're done. Installation is typically one day per zone.

For a triplex where each unit needs its own system, we install one outdoor unit per unit or, in some cases, a multi-zone system where one larger outdoor unit serves multiple indoor heads. The right configuration depends on your building's layout and how you manage your units.

The rebate process, step by step

Understanding the sequence helps you plan around it. Here's how it typically unfolds:

  1. Home assessment. We evaluate your current heating system, your building's insulation, and your typical energy bills. This tells us what size system you need and what savings to expect.
  2. System selection. We propose equipment that meets LogisVert's efficiency requirements and fits your budget. Written estimate before any commitment.
  3. Installation. Typically one to two days for a residential installation. We handle all the permits.
  4. Documentation. We provide the installation paperwork, equipment specs, and RBQ licence information needed for the rebate application.
  5. Rebate application. Either we submit on your behalf or we give you what you need to submit it yourself — depending on your preference and the current program process.
  6. Rebate payment. Hydro-Québec processes applications in the order they arrive. Typically 6–12 weeks from submission to receiving the payment.

When to install for the best experience

Spring and fall are the best installation windows. In July and August, heat pump demand spikes across Montreal and lead times on equipment and installation appointments can stretch. In November and December, the same thing happens as people realize their heating systems need upgrading. Spring — March through May — and fall — September and October — offer better lead times, more scheduler flexibility, and cooler outdoor temperatures that make the work more comfortable for everyone involved.

Thinking about the whole home while you're at it

A heat pump upgrade is often the right moment to think about other systems in the building. A properly functioning heat pump also protects against frozen pipes in Montreal winters by keeping the building consistently and efficiently heated through cold snaps — one less thing to worry about in January.

Some homeowners find it more efficient to address several things in one visit rather than scheduling separate trips over two years. We're honest about what makes sense to combine and what doesn't.

Frequently asked questions

How much is the LogisVert heat pump rebate in 2026?

The Hydro-Québec LogisVert program offers rebates up to approximately $7,840 for eligible heat pump installations. The exact amount depends on the system type, your home's heating needs, and the equipment selected. Amounts are updated periodically — confirm current rates at hydroquebec.com.

Do I need an RBQ-licensed contractor to get the LogisVert rebate?

Yes. Hydro-Québec's LogisVert program requires that the heat pump installation be performed by an RBQ-licensed contractor. Without that licence, the work doesn't qualify, and neither does the rebate. This is one of the main reasons to confirm your installer's credentials before signing anything.

Are mini-split heat pumps eligible for LogisVert rebates?

Yes, most ductless mini-split systems (mural and multi-zone) are eligible, as are central heat pump systems. The system must meet minimum efficiency ratings specified in the program. We help confirm eligibility before any work begins.

Who applies for the rebate — me or the contractor?

In most cases, the contractor handles the rebate application on your behalf, or you submit it after installation using the documentation they provide. We guide clients through the paperwork.

How long does the rebate process take?

Typically 6–12 weeks from installation to receiving the rebate payment. The timeline depends on the volume of applications Hydro-Québec is processing.

Can a plex or commercial building get the LogisVert rebate?

Rebate availability and amounts vary by property type. Residential (including plexes) and some commercial properties may qualify. Contact us to discuss your specific situation — we can tell you what to expect before any commitment.
