Frozen Pipes in Montreal: How to Prevent Burst Pipes This Winter

Montreal winters are predictable in one way: January will bring at least one stretch of −20°C or colder, often for several days in a row. Pipes in exterior walls, unheated crawl spaces, and cold garages can freeze within a few hours of those temperatures. When water freezes inside a pipe, it expands — and if the pressure has nowhere to go, the pipe splits. Here's what freezes first, how to stop it, and what to do if you're already dealing with a frozen line.

Which pipes freeze first in a Montreal plex

The short answer is: any pipe that's close to an exterior wall, poorly insulated, or in a space that isn't heated. In practice, that means:

  • Supply lines running inside exterior walls — common in Outremont and NDG homes where plumbing was added after original construction, often without proper insulation behind the pipe
  • Pipes in uninsulated crawl spaces under the ground floor of Plateau triplexes
  • Water supply lines to outdoor taps or garages in Verdun and Ahuntsic bungalows
  • The cold-water supply to the kitchen in older NDG duplexes, where it runs along an outside-facing wall

Hot-water pipes can freeze too, but they're less common because the water moving through them stays warmer. It's the cold supply lines in the coldest corners of the building that cause most of the calls we get in January.

The plex owner problem: vacant units

If you own a triplex and have a vacant unit going into winter — maybe a tenant moved out in December and you're showing the place — that unit needs to stay above 15°C even when no one lives there. This is an insurance requirement in Quebec. If a pipe bursts in an unheated vacant unit and your insurer finds out you let it drop below that threshold, your claim may be denied. Keep the heat on. It costs less than one burst pipe repair.

Prevention: the practical checklist

Most frozen pipe problems in Montreal are preventable with a few hours of attention before the cold arrives.

Insulate the vulnerable spots

Foam pipe insulation sleeves are available at any hardware store and take about 20 minutes to install on exposed pipes in a crawl space or utility room. If you have supply pipes running through an exterior wall cavity that isn't insulated, that's a harder fix — it usually means either opening the wall to add insulation or rerouting the pipe to an interior wall. We can inspect and tell you which approach makes sense for your building.

Keep heat consistent across all units

The heating in one unit affects the temperature of shared walls and floor/ceiling assemblies. A vacant, unheated unit can pull enough cold into a shared wall to freeze the pipes serving the unit above or below. Keep all units at least 15°C, even vacant ones.

Know where your main shut-off is

Before January, walk your basement and locate the main water shut-off valve for each unit. In many older Rosemont plexes, the shut-offs are in different corners of the basement and haven't been touched in 20 years. Find them now. If one is seized or broken, fix it before winter — a functioning shut-off is the first line of response when something goes wrong.

The dripping tap trick — when it's worth it

During an extreme cold snap (below −25°C for multiple nights), letting cold taps drip slowly on supply lines that run through exterior walls can prevent freezing. Moving water is harder to freeze than standing water. This is a last resort for truly extreme conditions, not something to do every winter night — but it's useful to know.

If a pipe freezes: what to do right now

Frozen doesn't necessarily mean burst. If you lose pressure to a tap in cold weather, here's how to handle it without making things worse:

  1. Locate your main shut-off valve first. If the pipe has already burst and you don't know this yet, finding the shut-off while water is spraying is harder than finding it now.
  2. Open the tap that's affected. Leave it open — this relieves pressure as the ice thaws and lets you see when flow returns.
  3. Do not turn on the hot water tap to thaw faster. The sudden pressure change can stress the pipe further.
  4. Apply gentle, direct heat if you can see the frozen section. A hair dryer on the pipe, or a heating pad wrapped around it, works well. Start at the tap end and work back toward the cold source. Never use an open flame — this is how plumbing repairs become house fires.
  5. If you can't locate the frozen section, or if the pipe looks swollen or has moved, stop and call a plumber. A burst you catch in the first hour is far cheaper than one you discover through a water stain on the ceiling a day later.

Signs a pipe has already burst

Sometimes a pipe bursts in a wall or ceiling and you don't know immediately. Watch for:

  • Sudden and unexplained drop in water pressure across the building
  • A new water stain on a ceiling or wall, especially after a cold night
  • Your water meter showing movement when every tap in the building is off
  • Sound of running water from inside a wall

If any of these appear, shut off the main supply and call us. The faster you act, the less drywall gets involved.

What repair costs look like in Montreal

A pipe burst in an accessible utility room — exposed copper or PEX in a mechanical room — is a straightforward repair. A few hundred dollars including parts and labour, usually done in a couple of hours. We give a written estimate before starting.

A pipe burst inside a finished wall is a different story. We fix the pipe, but the drywall opening, patching, and any flooring or ceiling damage is additional — and that's before you consider anything that was damaged by the water itself. Restoration costs can easily exceed the plumbing repair by a factor of five. Prevention genuinely is cheaper.

Frequently asked questions

At what temperature do pipes freeze in Montreal?

Pipes start freezing when indoor temperatures near the pipe drop below 0°C. In Montreal winters — regularly hitting −20°C or lower — exposed pipes in exterior walls, crawl spaces, and garages are at risk within hours.

Can I thaw a frozen pipe myself?

Sometimes. If you can see the frozen section and there's no sign of cracking or bulging, gentle heat from a hair dryer or heating pad (never an open flame) can work. If you can't locate the freeze or if the pipe has moved, call a plumber — a burst pipe causes far more damage than the service call.

What does burst pipe repair cost in Montreal?

Emergency repair cost depends heavily on where the burst is and how much drywall or flooring is involved. Visible pipe bursts in a utility room: a few hundred dollars. Pipes inside finished walls: water damage restoration adds up fast. Prevention is always cheaper.

Does home insurance cover frozen pipe damage in Quebec?

Most Quebec home insurance policies cover sudden water damage from a burst pipe — but only if you can show the home was properly heated. If you left a vacant plex unheated over winter, a claim may be denied.

How do plex owners in Montreal prevent frozen pipes?

Key steps: insulate exposed pipes in exterior walls and crawl spaces; keep the heat above 15°C in all units including vacant ones; drip cold water overnight in extreme cold; know where your main shut-off is. A plumber can inspect vulnerable spots in older plexes.
